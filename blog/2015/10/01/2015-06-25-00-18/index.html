Dear R,

in our hearts and in our thoughts, we will always be together, forever. And compared to infinity, two months does not feel that long... But you are right, having things to do helps a lot. For me, last week passed away quickly, because I had a conference. And I'm sure that once you are in China, time will pass faster for you. Thinking about it, I find myself in a paradoxical situation. On the one hand, when I think about you, I just feel a strong desire to meet you again, and time does not pass at all. On the other hand, the thought of you gives me motivation and energy to work really, really hard, and when I am completely absorbed in my work, then time does pass faster. So, in total, does time pass more quickly or more slowly?

Oh yes, that experience you are describing is an absolute classic, I think that every mathematician goes through these situations many many times. I remember once when I was doing my PhD, I thought I had a brilliant idea, which would solve a main problem of my thesis. So I made an appointment with my advisor the next morning. But later in the evening, when I thought about it again, my argument fell apart. So the meeting the next day was very embarrassing. Fortunately, my advisor was sympathetic and not angry with me :-) Some time after that, I did manage to solve the problem. And you can imagine that I checked everything many many times :-D Although these failures seem to lead nowhere, I think that they are actually quite important. We can learn a lot and gain some insights, in particular if we understand what exactly went wrong, and why.
