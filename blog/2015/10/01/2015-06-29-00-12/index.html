Dear R,

yes, I brought all this stuff to London, into my apartment :-D It was not only because I like my things, but there were actually practical reasons. One was that my new University in London agreed to pay for the moving expenses. And since I had lots of stuff (mostly books) anyway, I just asked a moving company with a really big car to transport some furniture as well. The other reason is that I have put all my furniture into the basement of my parents' house. But because I had quite a bit of stuff, our basement is really full. My mother joked that it looks like we are opening a furniture store :-) So I thought, it might be a good idea to at least bring a little bit of furniture with me.

Talking about moving, we moved quite a lot when I was small. And I didn't like it, because it meant saying farewell to friends, to familiar places. I remember once before one of our moves, I was looking out from our window and thinking to myself that this view is going to disappear, like all the previous ones, and it made me sad. Only when we came to Muenster did our moving activities stop. Maybe that's why I stayed in Muenster for such a long time :-) In academics, especially at the beginning of a career, it is very common to move around often. But this is a bit different, because I understand now that new environments also mean new chances. Still, for me, the idea of settling down at some place one day seems attractive.

S University is a very good place, no? From what I hear, apart from a good thesis, finding a postdoc position relies a lot on personal contacts and references. The subject matters as well, but I know quite a few colleagues who are happy to take postdocs working on slightly different, but related areas. And I also know several people who used the transition period from PhD to postdoc to go into new directions in research, starting their own journey through mathematics. So I guess apart from working on one's thesis, it is important to develop broad interests. In my case, after my PhD, my supervisor kept me as a postdoc. On the one hand, this spared me some trouble, on the other hand, I am missing this experience. But I can see the perspective of colleagues, who sometimes have open positions and desperately try to find good candidates. So there will be chances.

I listened to Les Petits Chanteurs de Saint Marc. Their singing is really beautiful. Their voices are so soft and tender. Like angels.

Tomorrow in a week, you are going back to China, aren't you? I'm thinking that maybe, if you are not too busy, we could skype at the end of next week so that I can wish you 'Bon voyage' :-)
