Words are wind? Maybe, but sometimes, words hurt more than swords. And some wounds never heal, they only scab.

Recently, I read a post in 知乎, which mentioned a notion, love map. It gives scores by your answers to questions related to your lover, in order to give an assessment of your relationship. One of the questions is whether you know all the closest friends of your lover. I didn’t give a second consider when I first saw this question: of course. Until the midnight between 25 and 26 August, I realized how wrong I was. I missed one.

Of course, I smelt something earlier, maybe you know which moment is that. Thanks to 知乎, I wanted to use your iPad to take a look at it that night. Thanks to my bad habit of closing all other apps when I use a certain one. So I noticed a mail when I used your ipad. I read it. That was X’s response to your mail on his birthday. I was shocked by some words in that mail. But I pretended that I saw nothing. And when I returned your iPad, I thought I saw something in your eyes. But I said nothing, too. Now, I think, maybe it would lead to a totally different result if I asked you about the mail or you decided to tell me the things by that chance.

I suppose you know, at that time, I trusted you through and through (Do you know where I learn this phrase? From Half-Blood Prince, it describes how Harry trusts Dumbledore). Like what you did when Clément’s friend said he hoped to see you again after your first meeting at that running match of pigs. (Though I thought your whole ignorance of him might be a little impolite.) So I didn’t pay any more attention to this. It only came to my mind again until your arrival in London.
